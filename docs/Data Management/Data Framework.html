Data Framework

Since the invention of computers, people have used the term data to refer to computer information, which was either transmitted or stored. But that‚Äôs not the only definition of data; there are other data types. So what are the dates? Data can be texts or numbers written on paper, or it can be bytes and bits in the memory of electronic devices, or it can be facts that are stored in a person‚Äôs mind.

The goal of many information systems is to transform data into information to generate knowledge that can be used for decision-making. To do this, the system must be able to take data, put the data into context, and provide tools for aggregation and analysis. A database is designed for just such a purpose.

Understanding the value of data quality from a reliable source is important. Quality data is key to making accurate, informed decisions. And while all data has some level of ‚Äúquality,‚Äù various characteristics and factors determine the degree of data quality (high-quality versus low-quality). Furthermore, different data quality characteristics will likely be more important to various organizational stakeholders.

Data Quality Framework‚Äã

To maintain the quality of data, we have chosen to use a data quality framework that is based on the following characteristics:

  • Accuracy - a key attribute of high-quality data, a single inaccurate data point can wreak havoc across the entire system.
  • Completeness - It is defined as a measure of the percentage of data that is missing within a dataset
  • Consistency - It ensures that the source of the information collection is capturing the correct data based on the unique objectives of the department or company.
  • Integrity - Integrity of data refers to the level at which the information is reliable and trustworthy.
  • Reasonability - It measures the degree to which the data is consistent with the organization‚Äôs objectives.
  • Timeliness - Timeliness measures how up-to-date or antiquated the data is at any given moment
  • Uniqueness/Deduplication - Uniqueness is a data quality characteristic most often associated with customer profiles.
  • Validity - Validity refers to information that fails to follow specific company formats, rules, or processes
  • Accessibility - Accessibility refers to the level of access to the data that is available to the organization.

Data Profiling‚Äã

Data profiling is the process of analyzing and understanding the structure, content, and quality of data. The goal of data profiling is to identify any issues or anomalies in the data that may impact its usefulness or accuracy.

Data profiling involves several steps, including data discovery, data analysis, and data quality assessment. Data discovery involves identifying the location and source of data, as well as the format and structure of the data. Data analysis involves examining the content of the data to identify patterns, relationships, and other features. Data quality assessment involves evaluating the accuracy, completeness, and consistency of the data.

Data profiling can be used for a variety of purposes, including data migration, data integration, and data cleansing. It can help organizations to identify data quality issues and take corrective action to improve the accuracy and usefulness of the data.

Data profiling can also be used to identify opportunities for data enrichment, which involves adding additional data to enhance the value and usefulness of the data. By improving the quality and completeness of their data, organizations can make more informed decisions, improve business operations, and deliver better products and services to customers.

The data profiling process consists of multiple analyses that work together to evaluate your data:

Key and cross-domain analysis‚Äã

During a key and cross-domain analysis job, your data is assessed for relationships between tables. The values in your data are evaluated for foreign key candidates and defined as foreign keys. A column might be inferred as a candidate for a foreign key when the values in the column match the values of an associated primary or natural key. If a foreign key is incorrect, its relationship with a primary or natural key in another table is lost. After completing a key and cross-domain analysis job, you can run a referential integrity analysis job on your data. Referential integrity analysis is an analysis you use to fully identify violations between foreign key and primary or natural key relationships. During a referential integrity analysis job, foreign key candidates are investigated concisely to ensure that they match the values of an associated primary or natural key.

Baseline analysis‚Äã

We run a baseline analysis job to compare a prior version of the results with the current results for the same data source. If differences between both versions are found, we can assess the significance of the change, such as whether the quality has improved.
