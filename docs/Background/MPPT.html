Malaria Prediction and Planning Toolkit (MPPT)

The malaria prediction and planning toolkit(MPPT) has AI/ML-driven data analytics and visualization capabilities to improve the quality and completeness of local health data, secure multiple sources of detailed historical weather data, and create a data visualization dashboard that integrates epidemiological, service delivery, and weather data to guide practical programmatic decisions for two of the highest-burden districts in Odisha State: Koraput and Malkangiri.

The strategic use of open source tools such as Python and proprietary software like Tableau and Altryx has been instrumental in refining the model, demonstrating the utility of the dashboard in the field, and story-tell through an established network. With the continued partnership, we can create processes to reengineer the input for data quality and further improve AI to inform highly utilizable dashboards that will help program managers and community health workers on the ground to have success in their job - whether to divert rapid diagnostic tests (RDTs) in the most inaccessible geographies in anticipation of extreme weather or to make real-time changes to their active surveillance based on predicted outbreaks, for example.

Planned Enhancement of the Malaria Prediction & Planning Tool (MPPT) As part of the next iteration of FHF, the partnership intends to continue the current modeling in Odisha State, as India serves as an excellent demonstration ground given its proximity to Southeast Asia, where resistance to malaria is a significant threat, as well as one of the world‚Äôs high burden countries that is making tremendous progress (along with India‚Äôs scientific breakthroughs on vaccines and genetically modified mosquito research).

The following are ways in which the MPPT data sources/input will be refined and enhanced in the next phase of the program:

  • Weather data resolution: MPPT is currently utilizing weather data at a sub-district level resolution and analyzing the surveillance data to provide forecasting insights. Soon, MPPT will begin using more precise weather data to create forecasting outputs that can be drilled down to target locations such as villages or hamlets rather than district-wide. The India Meteorological Department has also agreed to install additional sensors to create a better prediction and provide historical and forecasted weather data to provide sustainability to weather-based forecasting.

  • DISHA Module: Data Integrated Solutions for Healthy Futures with Artificial Intelligence (DISHA). DISHA is a one-stop module that will house all the methods required to explore, analyze, predict, and prescribe actions from climate and health information. DISHA enables to build the state-of-the-art advanced Artificial Neural Network algorithms ‚Äì and provides health authorities with the ability to precisely predict mosquitoborne disease prevalence and incidence by locality. DISHA is a cross-mythological term with deep roots in language, religion, culture, and literature. Later this year, DISHA would emerge as a pivot to balance the methods and functions to explore and analyze data in the two disciplines. The novelty of DISHA lies in its conceptualization as an interdisciplinary applied module. DISHA uses state-of-the-art deep-learning models to make forecasts up to the Sub-Center level and develop Stacked Ensembles of Long Short Term Memory Models to enable program managers to microplan the delivery of services up to Sub-Center levels based on the micro-forecasts.

  • Cloud-based automated Pipelines: We use Microsoft Azure-based data storage and data engineering pipelines. We use popular and secure services like Azure Blob Storage and Azure Data Factory to serve our purpose. For data collection, we have a data portal hosted on Azure app services to get health data directly from the data entry operators to the cloud storage, and then our automated pipelines (based on DISHA Module) clean and process the data to host it on the Snowflake database, which can then be accessed via SQL queries as per the need.

  • DISHA Android App: The volume of data would increase with real-time reporting as the frontline health workers with android devices enable daily reporting of malaria incidence from 100 subcenter hotspots (covering about 300,000 ‚Äì 500,000 population). With the input of this data, the volume of surveillance data in the 100 subcenters would expand by 30X (monthly to daily), in addition to requiring faster forecasting. The android app is currently in the Beta phase and is expected to launch in the last week of September 2022. The app has backend storage on Azure which can be directly linked to our main pipeline based on DISHA modules to get the real-time malaria data.

  • Altryx Workflows: The use of Altryx workflows, drag and drop solutions to data engineering, feature extraction, and data cleaning have created a standard procedure to clean and upload data to cloud servers for model building and dashboarding. The process will soon be shifted to a GUI-based open-source system backend on Python.

  • Big Data Analytics: With the increasing amount of data being generated by individuals and organizations, big data analytics has become a crucial tool for businesses looking to gain a competitive edge. Data bricks is a cloud-based platform that provides a unified analytics engine for big data processing. It enables businesses to perform complex data processing tasks quickly and efficiently, without the need for specialized hardware or software. Data bricks also provides a collaborative environment for data scientists, analysts, and other users to work together and share insights. Data bricks is designed to support a range of big data analytics use cases, including data warehousing, machine learning, and streaming analytics. Its platform is built on Apache Spark, an open-source distributed computing system that enables fast and scalable processing of large data sets.

  • Geospatial Data Utility: In partnership with Cognizant, MPPT will extract geospatial information to develop a database of health workers, facility locations, and ecological data. The use of advanced machine vision for pixel-level analysis to identify and analyze breeding sites, vegetation, and other factors responsible for malaria transmission will be used as multiband raster data to quantify risk and inform predictions. The raster data, along with weather forecasts modeled alongside malaria, will help in making accurate predictions of malaria.

  • DISHA Chatbot: DISHA is an AI-powered chatbot that provides information about malaria. It is a user-friendly and interactive tool designed to help individuals learn more about the symptoms, prevention, and treatment of malaria. DISHA is equipped with a natural language processing (NLP) system that allows it to understand and respond to user queries. Users can interact with DISHA via a chat interface and ask questions about malaria, including symptoms, diagnosis, and treatment options. DISHA provides accurate and up-to-date information about malaria and can also help users locate nearby clinics or hospitals for treatment. Additionally, DISHA can help users determine their risk of contracting malaria based on their location, travel history, and other factors.

Dashboard Development‚Äã

The future storyboarding through an open-source Dashboard using Dash by Plotly in Python and Tableau would focus on comprehensive yet insightful information drawn from different data sources to drive action. A detailed data visualization dashboard ensures an optimal user interface for maximum application in daily decision-making. The dashboard has been hardwired with a combination of primary records and derived data, key indicators used by the program managers for regular decision-making. The enhanced/refined dashboard has been conceived to consist of five wireframes laid out for two levels‚Äì planning (P) and operations (O).

  • Level P: Planning Dashboard: The planning dashboard will focus on empowering District and State level program managers in identifying geographic and population priorities for intensified interventions, stratifying them to tailor interventions as per the risk, incidence, and trends of infection, warning them before a focal upsurge of malaria turns into an outbreak, and prompting action in response to a more significant impact on a particular population. Providing a descriptive and comparative view of historical and predictive temperature, rainfall, and humidity patterns at district levels, informing monsoon-sensitive campaign planning. This will include three wireframes for Epidemiological Insights, Risk Stratification, and Early Warning and Mitigating Impact of Malaria on Vulnerable Population.

  • Level O: Operations Dashboard: The operations dashboard will focus on improving reporting quality, preventing under/non-reporting malaria cases, strengthening malaria surveillance, and promoting a seamless supply chain by supporting the operations related to Community Health Worker performance and recording stock management, and First Expiry First Out (FEFO), timely treatment and referral based on the insights and identify intensified monitoring targets to improve implementation fidelity at the grassroots. Showing variance in the historical timing of peaks of Pf (falciparum) and Pv (vivax) malaria, and predictive forecasts for the coming six months, along with testing and treatment requirements at the district level to prevent local stockouts and expiries. Here we have Current Operations Insights, and Prediction of Supply Needs as the two wireframes.

We also have a Knowledge center, which will host all the documentation and videos for smooth conduct.
